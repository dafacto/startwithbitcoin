Following is a curated list of Bitcoin-related resources:

Learning

  • Beginner Podcast Series — A great next-step in the learning process would be to listen to the podcast series of interviews put together by Peter McCormack, the What Bitcoin Did Beginner’s Guide to Bitcoin.
  • Intermediate Podcast Series — For a more in-depth introduction to cryptocurrencies and blockchains in general, be sure to listen to the Patrick O'Shaughnessy's Hashpower documentary.
  • Bitcoin as Money — If you’re interested in learning how to understand Bitcoin as money, be sure to read The Bitcoin Standard.

Buying, selling, earning interest & loans

  • Crypto.com — Crypto.com, an app for iOS and Android, allows you to buy and sell bitcoin, using bank transfer and credit card. They also have an interest earning savings account, a debit card allowing you to spend your bitcoin anywhere Visa is accepted, and allow you to use your bitcoin as collateral for a loan! If you use my referral code gpd9q473d2 you'll receive $50 on your first transaction.
  • BlockFi — BlockFi is a US-regulated company that allows you to buy and sell bitcoin via bank transfer, earn interest, and use your bitcoin as collateral for a loan.
  • Coinbase — Coinbase is a popular option for buying and selling bitcoin. Once you create an account and link your bank account, you can transfer in dollars to buy bitcoin, and then transfer out dollars if you sell bitcoin.
  • Nexo — Nexo is a company that allows you to earn interest on bitcoin, and use your bitcoin as collateral for a loan.

Do-it-yourself wallets

  • BRD Wallet — BRD is a popular do-it-yourself mobile wallet for iOS and Android that allows you to buy (but not sell) bitcoin using a credit card.
  • Ledger Hardware Wallet — Down the road, if you buy a lot of bitcoin, you might look into a highly secure do-it-yourself hardware wallet, such as the Ledger Nano. It pairs with the Ledger Live desktop computer software, that allows you to buy bitcoin (but not sell)
